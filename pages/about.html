Where do Cats Come From?

The domestic house cat, also known as FELIS CATUS, came from a linage of large cats, such as lions and tigers. The point in the evolutionary track where house cats and lions split appart is around 10-15 million years ago. This is also when a large amount of smaller cats diviated from the larger cats. The house cat is a part of the smaller cats, and is the only one that has been domesticated.

  • Large Pulipils - Where cats are nocturnal, and need to see in the dark.

  • Tail - Cats use their tails to help them balance, and to help them communicate with other cats.

Cat Habitats

House cats are found all over the world, and they live in a variety of habitats. Cats are incredably adaptable, historically they have been found in the desert, the jungle, and the tundra. Cats are also found in urban areas, where they are kept as pets. Cats are also found in the wild, where they are known as feral cats. Feral cats are cats that have been abandoned by their owners, and have had to adapt to living in the wild. Feral cats are a problem in many areas, as they can be a danger to local wildlife, and can spread disease.
